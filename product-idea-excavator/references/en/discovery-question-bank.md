# Product Discovery Question Bank

Do not dump this question bank on the user. Pick one high-leverage question at a time.

## Product Origin

- What specific moment made you think of this product?
- If you had to explain it to an engineer in one sentence, what is it?
- What is the painful or exciting moment behind the idea?
- Is this triggered by a real observation, your own pain, a customer request, or a technical possibility?

## Target User

- Who feels this pain most urgently?
- Who uses it, who pays, and who decides?
- What kind of user would try an imperfect early version?
- What does the user do today instead?
- What consequence does this user face if the problem remains unsolved?

## Demand Depth

- What is the strongest evidence that someone truly wants this?
- Is that evidence behavior, payment, workflow dependency, or just verbal interest?
- How often does the pain happen?
- What does the current workaround cost?
- Who would be upset if this disappeared tomorrow?

## Value Proposition

- What is the first moment when the user feels value?
- Does the product make something faster, cheaper, safer, more professional, or newly possible?
- If a competitor copied 80% of the features, what advantage remains?
- How would users describe the value in their own words?

## Product Flow

- What does the user see first?
- What steps complete the core task?
- Where might the user drop off or lose trust?
- What roles, permissions, notifications, or collaboration states are needed?
- What empty, loading, error, and recovery states exist?

## MVP

- If you could build only three features, which ones prove value?
- What should v1 intentionally not do?
- What is the smallest result someone would use or pay for this week?
- Can the value be validated manually, semi-manually, or with no-code tools first?
- If the user did not need to log in or integrate anything, what value could they still receive?

## AI And Technical Feasibility

- Is AI the core value or an efficiency layer?
- What are the AI inputs and outputs?
- What happens if the AI is wrong?
- Do users need sources, confidence, human review, or rollback?
- Is RAG, Agent workflow, tool calling, multimodal input, or fine-tuning actually needed?
- What data is required and who can access it?

## Technical Stack

- Should v1 be web, mobile, mini app, desktop, browser extension, chat bot, or API-first?
- What technology does the team already know?
- How fast does this need to launch?
- Are auth, payments, notifications, admin tools, real-time collaboration, or integrations required?
- Which technical decisions are reversible and which are hard to undo?

## Business And Growth

- Who pays and why?
- Where do the first 100 users come from?
- Does retention come from data, collaboration, habit, content, workflow, or results?
- Is subscription, usage-based pricing, transaction fee, service fee, or freemium more natural?

## Metrics

- What action proves the user experienced value?
- If you could track only three metrics after 30 days, what would they be?
- What metric might look good but fail to prove real value?
- For AI features, what accuracy, latency, cost, and correction-rate thresholds matter?

## Risk

- What is the most likely reason this product fails?
- Which assumption, if wrong, forces a strategy change?
- What is the biggest technical risk?
- What must be validated before the MVP?
- If we do nothing, will users still feel real pain?
